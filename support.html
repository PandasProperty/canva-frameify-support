How to Use Frameify

Frameify turns any image into a custom Canva frame. Here's how to get started:

Step 1: Open Frameify from your Canva Apps panel.
Step 2: Either select an image on your canvas and click "Use selected image", or upload a PNG with a transparent background.
Step 3: Preview the frame shape and click "Add frame to design".
Step 4: Drag any image onto your new frame to fill it!

Tips for Best Results

Use small images: Images under 500px produce the cleanest frames. Large, detailed images may need to be simplified to stay within Canva's shape path limits.

Use transparent backgrounds: Frameify traces the visible (opaque) area of your image. Make sure your image has a transparent background — remove the background first if needed.

Adjust precision: Use the precision slider to control the level of detail. Higher precision captures more detail but may simplify complex shapes to fit Canva's limits. Start with the Default setting and increase if needed.

Simple shapes work best: Clean silhouettes, icons, and simple shapes produce the best frames. Very intricate or detailed outlines may lose some detail.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Frameify free?

Yes, Frameify is completely free with no hidden costs, subscriptions, or premium features.

Are my images uploaded to a server?

No. All image processing happens locally in your browser. Your images never leave your device.

Why does my frame look like a rectangle?

This usually means the image doesn't have a transparent background. Make sure your source image is a PNG with the background removed.

Why is the frame shape not detailed enough?

Try increasing the precision slider. If the shape is very complex, Frameify may need to simplify it to stay within Canva's 2KB path limit. Using a smaller source image (under 500px) also helps.

The app says my design type is not supported. What do I do?

Frameify uses shape elements, which are not available in Canva Docs. Open a regular design (poster, presentation, social media post, etc.) to use Frameify.
